01Why We Exist

The gap between a device and a warning

Digital Shield was founded after the case of Simbi Sharon - a 17-year-old who died by suicide after falling victim to a sextortion scam - and countless quieter versions of the same story unfolding wherever a teenager carries a smartphone and a social media account. Cyberbullying, phishing, sextortion, and digital fraud rarely announce themselves. They arrive disguised as a new follower, a private message, a stranger who says the right thing.

Most parents hand over the device long before anyone hands over the orientation. Digital Shield exists to close that gap - with structured education, honest conversation, and a safe space for young people to ask the questions they're too afraid to ask at home.

"You bought them the phone. This guide gives them the shield." - Digital Shield Parent Cyber Safety Guide

Our Mission

To educate, protect, and empower young people to become responsible, resilient, and safe digital citizens through structured, age-appropriate cybersecurity education in schools.

02Programs

Two ways we show up

One program meets young people where they are. The other meets the institutions around them.

Flagship Program

Secondary 4 · Age ~16 · 3 Days, Annually

The Digital Shield Program

A structured, three-day cyber-safety and digital-literacy curriculum delivered inside secondary schools to Senior 4 students, right as they're gaining more independence online.

  1. 1
    Awareness & RealitiesThe digital landscape, real cases like Simbi Sharon's, understanding cyberbullying and sextortion, safe vs. unsafe online behavior.
  2. 2
    Knowledge & SkillsHow phishing and scams actually work, social media privacy and digital footprints, responding to a sextortion attempt, password and device hygiene.
  3. 3
    Empowerment & ActionRole-play scenarios, peer-led discussion and testimonial panels, how to support a friend in danger, reporting pathways, and a signed Digital Shield Pledge.

Delivered by trained, certified volunteer facilitators - many from Carnegie Mellon University Africa's cybersecurity program - through interactive workshops, gamified modules, and small-group mentorship, not lectures.

Institutional Program

Schools · TVETs · Health Centers · District Offices

Tech Umuganda

Named for umuganda, Rwanda's national tradition of community service, Tech Umuganda mobilizes volunteer tech talent - students and working professionals - to run structured "tech service days" inside the institutions that keep Rwanda running.

  1. 1
    AuditA standardized digital-health check: hardware condition, software issues, network and printer basics, security hygiene.
  2. 2
    FixOn-the-spot minor repairs - cleaning, malware removal, updates, backups, configuration - plus a vetted referral path for anything bigger than a volunteer visit.
  3. 3
    TeachShort digital-literacy micro-sessions on password hygiene, phishing awareness, and safe file habits, plus a written Facility Digital Health Report the institution keeps.

Every visit trains one on-site "Digital Steward," so the improvements outlast the volunteers who made them.

Why you can trust us with your support

Every donation and expenditure is documented and traceable.

Accountability
